Liquified Natural Gas (LNG)

Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG), as the name implies, is gaseous natural gas processed to remove certain components, typically to the point where it is 90 percent or more methane, and then cooled to the point of liquefaction. The end product is odorless, colorless, non-corrosive, and non-toxic. It is also much more dense than natural gas—taking up about 1/600th the space that the same amount of gaseous natural gas would take up. This makes LNG a preferred option for global energy trade. The considerable reduction in volume makes LNG much more efficient to transport and store than regular natural gas.

DRY BULK

AHI's dry bulk portfolio includes petroleum coke (Petcock) and elemental sulphur, mainly produced in the UAE. Both are by-products from oil and gas production and refining. Petcock is used globally as fuel in cement and power industries and in industrial applications like smelting, while sulphur is applied in industries such as fertiliser production, mining, and chemicals. These products contribute to various industrial processes and support global demand in key sectors. AHI continues to strengthen its position as a reliable supplier of these essential raw materials.
